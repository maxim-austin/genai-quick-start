# 3.3 Garbage in, garbage out: data products matter even more in the GenAI era

GenAI applications are only as good as the data they use. When data quality is poor – missing values, incorrect records, weak definitions, or outdated data – the AI will generate incorrect insights and answers. In practice, a Talk-to-your-Data GenAI app cannot work reliably across "any random dataset". It may work as an exception only for trivial or very well-structured datasets. For enterprise use cases, curated data products are the prerequisite for trustworthy GenAI outcomes.

## Recommendations

- **Use curated, domain-oriented data products** as the trusted foundation for analytics and AI. Ensure clear ownership and stewardship (business + technical) and embed operational characteristics such as data quality, freshness, lineage so teams and users can trust what the AI is using.
- **Treat platform limits** (e.g., semantic model size constraints in tools like Databricks Genie and Snowflake Cortex Analyst) as intentional trade-offs to balance reliability, complexity, latency, and cost. Use the data product concept to scope and structure the data needed for each domain. For example, if a tool supports 25 tables (like DBX Genie) in a semantic model, that is often sufficient for a typical domain data product. If you need substantially more, consider splitting the scope into multiple data products (and corresponding semantic models) rather than forcing everything into a single oversized model.
- **Remember that GenAI isn't a wizard** that creates correct analytics out of thin air – it's a tool that helps users reach their goals faster by automating parts of the work. Users remain responsible for validating the generated insights and acting on them appropriately. If an SME cannot produce a correct analytical result manually for a given business question – because the question is ambiguous or the underlying data quality is insufficient – an AI agent won't be able to produce a correct result either.
